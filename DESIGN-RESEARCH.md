# Design research: outsourced accounts payable services

Date: 2026-07-20  
Search intent: `outsourced accounts payable services`

## Five direct organic competitors reviewed

| Competitor | Page reviewed | Visual notes |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Personiv | https://www.personiv.com/ | Bold cobalt and violet fields, very large sans-serif headlines, monochrome people cutouts, geometric overlays, partner logos, service columns, and repeated orange CTAs. The look is memorable and human, but the carousel and immediate contact overlay compete with the AP message. |
| Invensis | https://www.invensis.net/services/outsourcing-accounts-payable | White and pale-blue split hero, dark navy type, polished office portrait, bright-blue quote CTA, three proof metrics, client-logo rail, and a numbered AP process. Clear and credible, though long, dense, and visually close to a standard BPO service page. |
| Auxis | https://www.auxis.com/ | Editorial consulting style with large clean headings, deep blue surfaces, warm accent color, restrained photography, and strong insight/case-study modules. It feels senior and controlled, but its broad advisory structure makes AP harder to find quickly. |
| Cogneesol | https://www.cogneesol.com/ | Corporate dark-blue system with a bright action color, large industry photography, certification marks, a dense mega-menu, and process-led finance content. The trust layer is strong; the page is busy and less welcoming than the best people-led competitor. |
| QX Global Group | https://qxglobalgroup.com/ | High-contrast enterprise outsourcing design with bold display type, people imagery, service bands, location/proof modules, and prominent consultation CTAs. Strong scale signal, but broad company positioning dilutes the invoice-to-approval story. |

## Selected inspiration

**Winner: Personiv**

Personiv has the strongest visual identity in the set. Its oversized headline, graphic image crop, saturated fields, and people-first photography make a finance outsourcing offer feel more approachable than the usual pale-blue corporate template.

## Patterns carried into the redesign

- One short, direct headline instead of a long SEO sentence.
- One strong people image in a custom geometric crop.
- Large editorial type and a small, disciplined color system.
- One repeated primary CTA: `Request an AP staffing plan`.
- Immediate AP task clarity under the headline.
- A simple responsibility ledger that shows where finance control stays in house.
- Service rows and process steps instead of a generic icon-card grid.
- Trust through concrete operating rules, not invented logos, reviews, or savings claims.

## Originality notes

The implementation is not a Personiv clone. It uses a warm paper background, dark teal, acid-lime, and invoice gold rather than cobalt/violet/orange. The serif display face, clipped ledger-page image frame, AP responsibility table, numbered service rows, and control-first copy were designed for this niche. No competitor assets, copy, logos, testimonials, metrics, or proprietary illustrations were reused.
